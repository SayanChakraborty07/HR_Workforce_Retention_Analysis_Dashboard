# Introduction

This project focuses on understanding the factors contributing to employee turnover at XYZ Company, which has been experiencing a consistent 15% exit rate. By analyzing HR data, the goal is to uncover patterns that help the organization make informed, data-driven decisions to improve retention.

# Project Overview

The dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of employee turnover using demographic, departmental, financial, and satisfaction-related variables. It highlights who is leaving, from which areas, and why, enabling management to identify critical issues affecting workforce stability.

# Key Performance Indicators

Employee Count – Total number of employees in the organization.

Turnover Count – Total number of employees who left.

Turnover Percentage – Percentage of exits from the overall workforce.

Active Employees – Total employees currently working.

Average Age – Mean age of employees.

# Visualizations Used:

Turnover by Gender – Shows exit patterns among male and female employees.

Department-wise Turnover – Highlights departments with the highest turnover, especially R&D.

Age-wise Turnover – Displays turnover frequency across age groups, with 26–35 showing highest exits.

Monthly Income Density Chart – Shows that employees with salaries below 60k have higher turnover.

Turnover by Job Role & Department – Identifies roles most vulnerable to turnover.

Job Satisfaction Analysis – Provides insights into satisfaction levels that may influence exits.

# Use in Real Life

Organizations can use such dashboards to identify high-risk employee segments, understand turnover drivers, track department performance, enhance engagement strategies, adjust compensation policies, and ultimately strengthen retention and workforce planning.

# Conclusion

This analysis gives a clear understanding of turnover patterns at XYZ Company and highlights the key factors leading employees to leave. The insights enable HR teams and management to take targeted actions, improve employee satisfaction, and reduce overall turnover for a more stable and productive workforce.
